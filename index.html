People

Dr Ciara McGrath is a Lecturer in Aerospace Systems at the University of Manchester, United Kingdom, with expertise in Astrodynamics and Space Mission Design. She is the Institution of Engineering and Technology (IET) Young Woman Engineer of the Year 2021, and has appeared on TV, radio, and podcasts, and given a TEDx talk, promoting engineering and the space industry to a wide audience. She has worked in the space industry in the Republic of Ireland, the United Kingdom, Germany, and the Netherlands, and in 2017 was a visiting Fulbright Scholar at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), USA.

Projects

SURROUND

SURROUND is a constellation of small satellites that will identify and track solar storms and solar radio bursts as they travel through the inner solar system. This innovative mission will provide us with early warnings and more accurate forecasts of the impacts of solar storms on Earth..

  • Space Weather
  • CubeSats
Project link
Safe Passage

Using satellite data from Sentinel-1 and Sentinel-2, Safe Passage maps dangerous estuaries to aid commercial, leisure and rescue craft navigating treacherous waterways worldwide.

  • Satellite data
Project link
GLAMIS

The Global Lidar Altimetry MISsion is bringing together expertise from the space and photonics sectors to pioneer a new approach to space-borne lidar; a system capable of mapping global topography and above-ground structure and change detection.

  • Lidar
  • Satellite constellations
SCARA

Satellite data for Condition monitoring & Archaeological Rapid Assessment is a collaborative project with Historic Environment Scotland investigating the suitability of PlanetScope and SkySat imagery for archaeological purposes in Scotland.

  • Archaeology
  • Satellite Data
