Towards a
Sustainable Space Future

We are developing sustainable approaches to space mission design that will provide vital data needed on Earth, whilst preserving our fragile space environment for generations to come.

Scroll Down

The University of Manchester (UoM) Space Sustainability Group , led by Dr Ciara McGrath , is a subdivision of the UoM Space Systems Engineering Research Group . Founded in 2024, our research topics include, mission design trade offs for environment benefit against impact, deep space sustainability, dark and quiet skies and space traffic management. We welcome interest from exceptional graduate students enthusiastic about ensuring a responsible future for humanity’s space activities. For all questions regarding ongoing projects, potential collaborations, PhD opportunities and speaking invitations contact ciara.mcgrath@manchester.ac.uk

People

Dr Ciara McGrath is a Lecturer in Aerospace Systems at the University of Manchester, United Kingdom, with expertise in Astrodynamics and Space Mission Design. She is the Institution of Engineering and Technology (IET) Young Woman Engineer of the Year 2021, and has appeared on TV, radio, and podcasts, and given a TEDx talk, promoting engineering and the space industry to a wide audience. She has worked in the space industry in the Republic of Ireland, the United Kingdom, Germany, and the Netherlands, and in 2017 was a visiting Fulbright Scholar at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), USA.

John Mackintosh is a postgraduate researcher at the University of Manchester focused on developing sustainable design approaches for future space missions. Now in the second year of his PhD, he was named a finalist for the British Interplanetary Society (BIS) Student Paper Competition 2023 for his work on space mission sustainability scoring, and presented his work on trade offs between data benefits and orbit environment effects in mission design at the 74th International Astronautical Conference (IAC). Most recently, he led a seminar on space mission-related Earth pollution and life cycle assessment for the Manchester Sustainable Futures Seminar Series, underscoring his commitment to advancing responsible space activity.

Projects

SURROUND

SURROUND is a constellation of small satellites that will identify and track solar storms and solar radio bursts as they travel through the inner solar system. This innovative mission will provide us with early warnings and more accurate forecasts of the impacts of solar storms on Earth..

  • Space Weather
  • CubeSats
Project link
Safe Passage

Using satellite data from Sentinel-1 and Sentinel-2, Safe Passage maps dangerous estuaries to aid commercial, leisure and rescue craft navigating treacherous waterways worldwide.

  • Satellite data
Project link
GLAMIS

The Global Lidar Altimetry MISsion is bringing together expertise from the space and photonics sectors to pioneer a new approach to space-borne lidar; a system capable of mapping global topography and above-ground structure and change detection.

  • Lidar
  • Satellite constellations
SCARA

Satellite data for Condition monitoring & Archaeological Rapid Assessment is a collaborative project with Historic Environment Scotland investigating the suitability of PlanetScope and SkySat imagery for archaeological purposes in Scotland.

  • Archaeology
  • Satellite Data
